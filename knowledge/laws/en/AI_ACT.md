# EU Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act)

Compact compliance-engine summary of selected AI Act provisions relevant to compliance systems and AI-enabled services.

## Art. 6 — Classification rules for high-risk AI systems

**Core content:** Article 6 defines when an AI system is considered high-risk, including systems that are safety components or fall within listed high-risk use cases. Classification depends on intended purpose, sector, and potential impact on fundamental rights, safety, or access to essential services.

**Website compliance relevance:** Compliance platforms using AI to assess legal risks should document intended purpose and evaluate whether outputs affect rights or legal positions. Classification determines whether stricter governance, documentation, and monitoring duties apply.

## Art. 13 — Transparency and provision of information to deployers

**Core content:** High-risk AI systems must be designed and accompanied by instructions enabling deployers to understand and use the system appropriately. Information must include characteristics, capabilities, limitations, expected performance, and foreseeable risks.

**Website compliance relevance:** AI compliance tools should provide clear explanations of model limitations, confidence, data dependencies, and appropriate use. Users need enough information to avoid over-reliance on automated legal assessments.

## Art. 14 — Human oversight

**Core content:** High-risk AI systems must be designed to allow effective human oversight during use. Oversight measures should enable humans to understand outputs, monitor operation, intervene, disregard outputs, or stop the system where necessary.

**Website compliance relevance:** Compliance engines should keep legal review loops for high-impact recommendations and allow manual confirmation before applying fixes. Automated remediation should include safeguards, audit trails, and override options.

## Art. 50 — Transparency obligations for providers and deployers of certain AI systems

**Core content:** Providers and deployers of specified AI systems must inform people when they interact with AI systems or when content is artificially generated or manipulated, subject to defined exceptions. Transparency duties aim to make AI involvement clear.

**Website compliance relevance:** Websites using chatbots, AI-generated legal notices, or AI-generated content should disclose AI involvement where required. Compliance interfaces should label AI-generated recommendations and distinguish them from verified legal advice.
