# Digital Services Act (DSA)

Compact compliance-engine summary of selected DSA provisions relevant to online services and website operators.

## Art. 14 — Notice and action mechanisms

**Core content:** Hosting service providers must provide easy-to-access, user-friendly mechanisms that allow individuals or entities to notify allegedly illegal content. Notices must be processed in a timely, diligent, non-arbitrary, and objective manner.

**Website compliance relevance:** Platforms with user-generated content need clear reporting flows, structured notice forms, and internal handling processes. Terms and moderation policies should explain how notices are reviewed and what actions may follow.

## Art. 15 — Statement of reasons and transparency reporting

**Core content:** Providers must give affected recipients clear reasons when they restrict content, accounts, visibility, monetisation, or access based on illegality or terms violations. Decisions should be transparent and include available redress options.

**Website compliance relevance:** Online platforms should generate moderation notices explaining the rule breached, the facts relied upon, and appeal options. Missing explanations increase regulatory and user-rights risk.

## Art. 24 — Online advertising transparency

**Core content:** Online platforms presenting advertisements must ensure users can identify that information is an advertisement, on whose behalf it is presented, and meaningful information about the main targeting parameters. Transparency must be available directly and in real time.

**Website compliance relevance:** Websites displaying targeted ads or sponsored content need ad labels, advertiser identity, and targeting disclosures. Dark patterns or hidden commercial intent create compliance risks.

## Art. 26 — Recommender systems

**Core content:** Online platforms using recommender systems must explain the main parameters used and provide information on options for users to modify or influence those parameters. Very large platforms have additional obligations.

**Website compliance relevance:** Services ranking feeds, products, search results, or content recommendations should disclose ranking criteria and user controls. Transparency notices should be accessible from the interface where recommendations appear.
